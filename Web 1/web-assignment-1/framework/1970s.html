Robin Hood

The Synopsis

The animated film of Robin Hood was released November 8,1973, it was Disney’s 21st animated feature film. The animated film of Robin Hood is narrated by the rooster Alan-a-Dale, who explains the tale of how Robin Hood and Little John who live in Sherwood Forest, who help the folks of Nottingham fight against the excessive taxation of a greedy and immature Prince John and wins the hand of the beautiful Maid Marian.

Characters and Voice Cast

  • Robin Hood (Protagonist) - Brian Bedford
  • Little John (Robin’s friend & sidekick) - Phil Harris
  • Prince John (Villain) - Peter Ustinov
  • Sir Hiss (Prince John’s loyal companion) - Terry-Thomas
  • Friar Tuck (friend of Robin) – Andy Devine
  • Skippy (friend of Robin) - Billy Whitaker
  • Maid Marian (Robin’s childhood sweetheart) - Monica Evans
  • Lady Kluck (Maid Marian’s attendant) - Carole Shelley

Movie Facts

Robin Hood grossed $9.6 million in rentals in Canada and the United States. The song “Love” was nominated for Best Original Song at the 46th Academy Awards but lost to “The Way We Were” from the film of the same name.
