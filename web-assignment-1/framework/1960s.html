101 Dalmations

The Synopsis

101 Dalmatians was released January 25, 1961, it was Disney’s 17th animated feature film. The film was based on the book by Dodie Smith from 1956 novel. The animated film tells the tale of a villainous women, Cruella De Vil who kidnaps a litter of dalmatian puppies who wants to use their fur to make coats. The puppy’s parents, Pongo and Perdita set out to rescue their children, in addition save another eighty-four puppies that were purchased from pet stores.

Movie Facts

101 Dalmatians grossed over $14 million dollars in Canada and the United States during its initial run.

In its initial release, the film received critical acclaim from critics, many of whom hailed it as the studio's best release since Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs (1937) and the closest to a real "Disney" film in many years.
